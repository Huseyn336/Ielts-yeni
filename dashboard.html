Exam Rules

Attention

  • Time management - There is a set time for each section. When time is up, the system will automatically move to the next stage.
  • Quiet environment - Take the exam in a quiet environment free from distractions.
  • Internet connection - Make sure you have a stable internet connection.
  • Registration information - Only log in with your own account, otherwise your result will be canceled.
  • Answer confirmation - Don't forget to click the "Confirm" button after selecting each answer.
  • Writing section - Write only in English. If plagiarism or automatic translation is detected, the result will be invalid.
  • Speaking section - Your camera and microphone must be active. Responses will be recorded as video and audio.

Prohibited

  • Using phones, tablets or other electronic devices.
  • Communicating with other participants during the exam.
  • Recording audio, taking photos or screenshots.
  • Using notebooks or additional materials (only tools available on the exam platform can be used).
  • Logging out or leaving the page (the exam will end automatically).
  • Sharing answers or distributing exam questions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What documents are required for the exam?
When coming to the exam, you must bring your ID card (identity card, driver's license or passport). Your student ID and exam registration documents may also be required.
How long does the exam last?
The IELTS exam lasts a total of 2 hours and 45 minutes. The Listening (30 min), Reading (60 min), and Writing (60 min) sections are conducted consecutively. The Speaking section may be conducted at a separately scheduled time.
When will I get the results?
Results for paper-based exams are usually ready within 13 days. Results for computer-based exams are ready within 3-5 business days. Results can also be checked online.
Can I retake the exam?
Yes, you can retake the IELTS exam as many times as you want. There is no restriction between exams. You must register again and pay for a repeat exam.
